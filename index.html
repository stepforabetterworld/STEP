About Us

STEP For A Better World is an NJ-based, student-led nonprofit community organization established in 2013 and inaugurated as a Leo Club in 2019. Sponsored by the West Windsor Lions Club and affiliated with Lions Club International, a global humanitarian organization, the organization is dedicated to serving the underprivileged through service projects, collection drives, and fundraisers while empowering youth to serve selflessly. Our members range from elementary school students to those with advanced degrees across NJ, and our service has spanned 12 countries globally.

      Mission        

STEP For A Better World LEO Club is committed to empowering youth and transforming communities through compassionate, strategic service. Our mission is to cultivate a generation of socially conscious leaders who understand that meaningful change begins with individual action and collective commitment. We believe in creating opportunities for young people to develop critical life skills, engage in impactful community service, and become catalysts for positive social transformation. By providing structured learning environments and meaningful service opportunities, we aim to inspire our members to look beyond themselves and recognize their potential to make a tangible difference. Our approach integrates personal growth with community development, ensuring that each project not only addresses immediate needs but also builds long-term capacity for sustainable change. We are dedicated to bridging gaps, breaking barriers, and building connections across diverse communities. Through partnerships with local and global organizations, we strive to address critical social challenges, promote understanding, and create pathways for hope and progress. Our ultimate goal is to nurture a compassionate, engaged, and proactive generation that sees service not as an obligation, but as a fundamental expression of human solidarity.


Vision

We strive to be at the forefront of innovative service projects, from large-scale meal packaging events to collection drives that surpass previous goals and expand our outreach to world record-breaking initiatives. Our consistent aim is to address pressing needs and inspire others to join our cause. By fostering a culture of continuous learning, leadership development, and hands-on community engagement, we aim to nurture generations of socially conscious individuals who see challenges as opportunities to make a difference.

Safety

We ensure that all safety precautions are met and promote a safe, fun, inclusive environment.

Training

We train our members to not only be their best selves but also the future leaders of tomorrow by further developing their interpersonal skills and leadership abilities. Our program focuses on fostering personal growth, encouraging proactive engagement, and mentorship.

Education

We educate and encourage our volunteers, members, and those in our communities to become lifelong learners and active participants in positive change. Our educational approach extends beyond traditional learning, emphasizing personal development, community engagement, and social awareness.

Partnership

We strategically partner with diverse organizations to establish meaningful relationships that amplify our community service impact and advance our vision of creating positive social change. By collaborating with local, national, and global partners, we leverage collective expertise, resources, and networks to address critical community needs more effectively and comprehensively.

150,000 Meal Packaging Event

​In under 7 hours we packed 150,000 meals in partnership with Liquid Church in Mercer County for Rise Against Hunger!

Guinness World Record of Longest Chain of Eyeglasses

On May 1, 2022, STEP assisted in Breaking the Guinness World Record utilizing 100,000 pairs of eyeglasses to create the longest chain of eyeglasses 13,030 m ( 42,749 ft and 4.13 inches) .

In 2023, STEP raised funds to purchase and disperse 202 backpacks filled with 18 different items!

Toy Drive

Janitorial Staff Tribute to Heroes

In 2020, STEP raised money to help the unnoticed but hard working Janitorial Staff of Princeton Hospital.

2023 Book Drive

In 2023, STEP raised money and 4323 books to donate to shelters, schools, and others!
